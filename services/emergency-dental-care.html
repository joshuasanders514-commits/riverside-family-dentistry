What counts as a dental emergency?

Any situation causing significant pain, swelling, bleeding, or trauma to your teeth or mouth is a dental emergency. A severe toothache, a knocked-out tooth, a broken tooth with sharp edges, or swelling that is spreading to your face or neck are all situations that need same-day attention. If you are unsure, call us and describe your symptoms. We will tell you exactly how urgently we need to see you.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions about Emergency Dental Care.

What should I do if I knock out a tooth?

Act quickly. Pick up the tooth by the crown (the top part), not the root. If it is dirty, rinse it gently with water but do not scrub it. If possible, place it back in the socket and hold it there. If you cannot do that, put it in a container of milk or hold it between your cheek and gum. Call us immediately. The chances of saving a knocked-out tooth are highest within the first 30 to 60 minutes.

Yes, absolutely. We welcome new emergency patients even if you have never been to our practice before.
